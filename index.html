About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Northwestern University Center for Robotics and Biosystems. I am a member of the Interactive and Emergent Autonomy Laboratory, and am advised by Dr. Todd D. Murphey. I received my B.S. in Engineering from MIT in 2016 and my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University in 2019.

I am an interdisciplinary scientist and engineer focusing on understanding and controlling cooperative agents. In my research, I explore how to algorithmically facilitate communication and adaptation for effective human-agent and agent-agent cooperation.
